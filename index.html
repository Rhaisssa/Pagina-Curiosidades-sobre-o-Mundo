Discover the 5 most beautiful and unknown places in the world

Snake Island

Queimada Grande Island, located 35 kilometers off the coast of São Paulo, is one of the most dangerous and restricted places on the planet. It was discovered in 1532 by the colonizing expedition of Martim Afonso de Souza. It is estimated that there may be between one and five snakes per square meter of the island. Due to the high risk that this situation represents, the Brazilian government has prohibited people from entering the island. The exception is only for authorized researchers, who, in order to conduct studies on site, must follow strict safety protocols and always be accompanied by a doctor.

The French cave of Lascaux

Com cerca de 17 mil anos, esta caverna fica localizada no sul da França é um dos exemplos mais bem preservados de arte pré-histórica no planeta. São cerca de 600 pinturas e mil gravuras estampadas nas paredes rochosas do local. Oito anos depois de sua descoberta, em 1940, a caverna de Lascaux foi aberta ao público. Em 1963 as visitas foram suspensas, pois a entrada de turistas afetou a caverna, provocando o crescimento de fungos e micróbios. Quase 60 anos depois, visitas à caverna continuam altamente restritas para evitar a destruição permanente da arte rupestre. Por isso, réplicas foram construídas nas proximidades para que os turistas possam conhecer as pinturas históricas."

Jiangsu National Security Education Museum

The Jiangsu National Security Education Museum in China houses a collection of top-secret documents about the country's history of espionage. Foreigners are not allowed to enter the museum. Photography is also not allowed inside the museum, even for Chinese citizens who are authorized to visit.

Area 51

Area 51 is a region that houses one of the most important US Air Force bases. It is located in the Nevada desert, 135 kilometers north of Las Vegas. Everything that goes on inside is extremely secret, and this secrecy has helped fuel many conspiracy theories, especially those related to extraterrestrials. Access is strictly prohibited, despite dozens of people trying to get there every year.

North Sentinel Island

The mystery surrounding the tribe living on North Sentinel Island is so great that no one even knows what language its inhabitants speak, or how many there are. What is known is that this is one of the last groups in the world that has no contact with outside civilization. The island, part of the Andaman and Nicobar archipelago in the Bay of Bengal in the Indian Ocean, is more than a thousand kilometers from any port, the closest one on the Indian mainland. Visits by outsiders are prohibited, mainly due to the risk of contaminating the natives with diseases from outside. In addition, the Sentinelese, as the island's inhabitants are called, are known for protecting their territory and attacking intruders.
